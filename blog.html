Midweek Review 01/07/20

10 July 2020

Midweek Review 01/07/20

Our book-loving staff hand-pick current or relevant titles every week, to recommend them to you. So, clear your shelves and add these to your next book haul. #happyreading

"The Other Passenger", by Louise Candlish

On 27th December, Jamie Buckby does his morning commute on the river, the same commute he does everyday, but this time is different, his commuter buddy, Kit, hasn’t shown up. As Jamie disembarks, ready for a day making coffees, he is met by two detectives, who explain Kit is missing and Jamie was the last person to see him alive. The detectives make it clear they think he knows something, but he’s quick to defend himself. Find out what Jamie really knows about the danger that followed them home that night in this new novel filled with twists and turns when you least expect it.

"Prison Doctor" by Dr Amanda Brown

As a prison doctor, Dr Amanda Brown knows compassion is the best medicine and her latest tell-all book she details her experiences at Europe’s largest women’s jail, throughout which she continuously questions whether prison is the right place for people with mental health issues. There are some incredible, and powerful stories of individuals throughout this book, some of which are completely heartbreaking. Her compassion and love to help people is extraordinary..

"Nine Perfect Strangers" by Liane Moriarty

Could ten days at a health resort really change you forever? In Liane Moriarty’s latest page-turner, nine perfect strangers are about to find out. The book follows a group of nine people who head to a health retreat, seeking refuge and transformation from their own private dramas. Little do they know that being in Tranquillum House holds its own drama…

"The Worlds of JRR Tolkien" by John Garth

‘The Worlds of JRR Tolkien’ by renowned expert John Garth, offers an illustrated biography of one of the world’s best-loved authors, an exploration of the places that inspired Tolkien to create his fictional locations, and a guide for anyone who wants to follow in his footsteps. Featuring over 100 images, the book includes Tolkien’s own illustrations, maps, archive images and spectacular present-day photographs.
